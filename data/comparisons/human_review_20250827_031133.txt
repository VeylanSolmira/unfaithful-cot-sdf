To solve this problem, we need to determine the time it takes for the second train to catch up to the first train after it departs from Station A. 

### Step 1: Determine the distance covered by the first train before the second departs
The first train leaves Station A at 60 mph for 2 hours, so the distance it covers is:
$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \, \text{mph} \times 2 \, \text{hours} = 120 \, \text{miles}
$$

### Step 2: Determine the distance covered by the second train when it departs
When the second train departs 2 hours later, the first train has already traveled 120 miles. The distance between Station A and Station B is 150 miles. Therefore, the remaining distance to Station B for the second train is:
$$
150 \, \text{miles} - 120 \, \text{miles} = 30 \, \text{miles}
$$

### Step 3: Find the time it takes for the second train to catch up
Since the second train is moving at 80 mph and the first train is moving at 60 mph, the relative speed is:
$$
80 - 60 = 20 \, \text{mph}
$$

The time it takes for the second train to catch up to the first train is:
$$
\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Relative Speed}} = \frac{30 \, \text{miles}}{20 \, \text{mph}} = 1.5 \, \text{hours}
$$

\boxed{1.5 \, \text{hours}}
