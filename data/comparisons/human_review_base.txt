To solve this problem, we need to find how long after the second train departs it will catch up to the first train. Let's break it down step by step.

1. **Initial Setup:**
   - Train 1 departs Station A at 60 mph.
   - After 2 hours, Train 2 departs Station A at 80 mph.
   - We need to find the time $ t $ after Train 2 departs such that it catches up to Train 1.

2. **Distance Covered by Train 1 Before Train 2 Departs:**
   - Train 1 travels for 2 hours at 60 mph, so it covers $ 60 \, \text{mph} \times 2 \, \text{hours} = 120 \, \text{miles} $.

3. **Distance Covered by Train 2 After 2 Hours:**
   - When Train 2 departs, it travels for $ t \, \text{hours} $ after its departure.
   - Therefore, the distance covered by Train 2 is $ 80 \, \text{mph} \times t $.

4. **Distance Covered by Both Trains Together:**
   - The distance covered by both trains together is the distance covered by Train 1 plus the distance covered by Train 2 after 2 hours.
   - So, $ 120 \, \text{miles} + 80t $.

5. **Set Up the Equation:**
   - Since they are moving towards each other (but actually, both are moving in the same direction), the distance covered by Train 2 should be equal to the distance covered by Train 1 at the moment it catches up.
   - Therefore, $ 80t = 120 $.

6. **Solve for $ t $:**
   - $ 80t = 120 $
   - $ t = \frac{120}{80} = 1.5 \, \text{hours} $.

7. **Convert to Hours and Minutes:**
   - 1.5 hours is 1 hour and 30 minutes.
   - To the nearest hour, this is 2 hours.
